Digital Health Debate: Millennials and Generation Z

The reason for discussing the bad health effects of social media is that it is linked to a variety of mental health issues, and to further enlighten people's pre-existing awareness about it. Social media may reinforce the brain's reward system and produce dopamine, a "feel-good chemical" associated with pleasurable activities. Excessive social media use, on the other hand, can lead to feelings of isolation, loneliness, difficulties sleeping, physical health issues, harassment, excessive expectations, and distraction.

Social media has evolved into an essential part of modern life, allowing us to easily communicate with others as well, meet other individuals, and share information. However, several harmful social media impacts have been identified. Some of the negative effects of social media include online harassment, the dissemination of incorrect information, addiction, a decline in face-to-face connection, low self-esteem, social isolation, division, negativity, online stalking as well as assault, a decrease in privacy, comparison, and envy.

It provides insight into the difficulties both old and new generations experience as a result of excessive social media use. This knowledge can enable them support and assist younger generations as they navigate the online world. It serves as a cautionary tale for the generation after Gen Z, emphasizing the potential downsides of excessive social media use and the importance of maintaining a healthy balance.
